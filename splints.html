Immobilization Techniques: Splints

A focused visual guide to common upper and lower extremity splinting required during an on-call shift, emphasizing material use and proper positioning.

Volar Forearm Splint

Primary Use: Distal radius fractures, carpal injuries, soft tissue wrist injuries.
Key Tip: Ensure adequate padding over the ulnar styloid and maintain a slight wrist extension (10-20 degrees).

View Step-by-Step Visual Guide

Sugar-Tong Splint

Primary Use: Elbow and forearm fractures (e.g., highly unstable distal radius or both bone forearm fractures).
Key Tip: Prevents pronation/supination. The elbow must be maintained at 90 degrees of flexion.

View Application Video

Posterior Long-Leg Splint

Primary Use: Tibia fractures, unstable ankle fractures, knee dislocations (temporary).
Key Tip: Include the entire foot and maintain the ankle in neutral dorsiflexion. Must be well molded to the calf and heel.
